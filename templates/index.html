    Circuit Limits Explained

    The exchange sets upper and lower price boundaries each morning based on the previous close. If a stock hits its upper circuit, it cannot trade above that level for the rest of the day — preventing panic buying. The lower circuit stops panic selling. The ±20% here is SEBI's standard rule for most stocks.
